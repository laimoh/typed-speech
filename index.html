What if a word actually looked like how you said it?

?

Do our usual ways of communication do justice to bring out intended meaning?

Sound is not just an important component of communication but also an identifier of the person carrying it. Different people say the same word differently, the human ear can percieve these meanings quite well. However our writing tools lack these qualities of communication. This project is an experimental typeface generator. It detects the words being said and analyses the frequencies of sound as it was spoken.

* An internet connection & microphone input is required for this to work.
